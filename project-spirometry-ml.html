Overview

Central Thesis

Standard pulmonary function testing (PFT) interpretation focuses almost exclusively on expiratory indices — FEV₁, FVC, FEV₁/FVC — while the inspiratory limb of the flow–volume loop is systematically overlooked. This project challenges that asymmetry through two parallel lines of investigation: image-based CNN classification of flow–volume loop morphology (Study 1) and numerical spirometric phenotyping of PRISm patients (Study 2).

Both studies converge on the same conclusion: the inspiratory limb encodes clinically actionable diagnostic signals that current PFT guidelines do not capture.

Study 1

CNN Classification of Flow–Volume Loop Morphology

PROBLEM

Upper airway obstruction (UAO) and inducible laryngeal obstruction (ILO/VCD) are frequently misdiagnosed as asthma because their hallmark features — inspiratory plateau, reduced PIF, saw-tooth oscillations — are confined to the inspiratory limb and assessed by subjective visual inspection with poor inter-observer reliability (κ = 0.12).

DATA

154 de-identified flow–volume loop images from Taipei Medical University Hospital, annotated by experienced pulmonologists into 6 morphological classes. Due to severe class imbalance (minority classes B/D/E: 3–5 images each), the task was restructured as binary classification:

Study 2

Numerical PRISm Spirometric Phenotyping

"Preserved Ratio Impaired Spirometry Is Independently Associated with an Inspiratory-Limited Physiological Profile: A Longitudinal Cohort Study." Submitted to Thorax (BMJ) — currently under review. Sole first author; corresponding author Pai-Chien Chou, MD, PhD (Division of Pulmonary Medicine, TMUH).

PROBLEM

PRISm (FEV₁ < 80% predicted, FEV₁/FVC ≥ 0.70) is treated as a homogeneous entity in current guidelines. Emerging evidence suggests a subset may arise from upstream inspiratory flow limitation — an "inlet limitation" hypothesis — rather than intrinsic parenchymal pathology. This subtype is invisible to conventional expiratory-focused analysis.

DATA

57,295 spirometry records from TMUH (Dec 2007 – May 2026). After inclusion criteria and best-trial selection:

CohortNDescription
Cross-sectional7,187Unique individuals, all eligible visits
Longitudinal1,552≥2 distinct visit dates (21.6% of cohort)

Longitudinal phenotypes: Stable PRISm (n=412), Intermittent PRISm (n=327), Never PRISm (n=813). UAW proxy defined as ≥2 of: PIF below sex-adjusted median; PIF/PEF below sex-adjusted median; FIV₁/FIVC below sex-adjusted median.

1

2

3

47.4% of Intermittent PRISm individuals transitioned to non-PRISm status between first and last visits, indicating reversibility and a potential intervention window.

2b · Supervised ML Classification (Expiratory Features Only)

To test whether UAW proxy positivity is recoverable from expiratory data alone, three classifiers were trained with all inspiratory components strictly excluded (no PIF, PEF, FIV₁, FIVC in features).

Near-chance AUC (0.557–0.568) is a positive finding, not a failure. It demonstrates that expiratory indices cannot reconstruct inspiratory-limited physiology — the inspiratory limb encodes information orthogonal to what expiratory measurements capture. XGBoost feature importance was evenly distributed across FEV₆, FVC, FEV₁ (each ≈0.10), confirming no leakage.

2c · Unsupervised GMM Clustering

A Gaussian Mixture Model was applied to inspiratory indices (PIF, PIF/PEF, FIV₁/FIVC) in the longitudinal subgroup (n=1,464 with complete inspiratory data) to test whether a natural inspiratory-limited subpopulation exists independent of any expiratory labelling.

★

Two-cluster solution selected by BIC: inspiratory-limited cluster (n=443, 30.3%) vs normal-inspiratory cluster (n=1,021, 69.7%). Bootstrap silhouette coefficient: 0.216 ± 0.026.

★

The inspiratory-limited cluster was significantly enriched for Stable PRISm individuals, with systematically lower PIF and FIV₁/FIVC — a bimodal structure not derivable from expiratory features alone.

The GMM finding complements the supervised null result: the inspiratory-limited phenotype is a genuine, separable physiological entity — not an artefact of arbitrary threshold selection.

Limitations & Future Work

What's Next

  • Image dataset (n=154) with severe minority-class imbalance limits multi-class classification. No public labelled UAO dataset exists for external validation.
  • The two datasets come from different patient populations and cannot be linked at the individual level — true multimodal fusion (CNN embeddings + numerical indices) requires prospective paired data collection.
  • The UAW proxy is defined by spirometry-derived indices without laryngoscopic confirmation; findings are hypothesis-generating rather than confirmatory.
  • Future directions: expand image dataset for rare classes; collect region-level loop annotations for object detection; pursue paired image + numerical data for hybrid multimodal architecture; external validation with laryngoscopy/bronchoscopy labels.
